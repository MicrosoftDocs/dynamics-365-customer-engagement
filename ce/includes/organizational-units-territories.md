**Organizational units** define who is responsible for the work. Use them to separate teams to control access and operations.

**Territories** define where the work happens. Use territories to route work geographically to optimize scheduling and travel.

Together, organizational units and territories help dispatchers assign work to the correct resources.

For example, David is a technician at Contoso Services, an HVAC and plumbing company in the Seattle area. David's organizational unit is the HVAC division and territory is the Bellevue region.

Avery is the dispatcher for Contoso Services and uses organizational units and territories to assign work to the appropriate technicians.
