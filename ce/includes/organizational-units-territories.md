**Organizational units** define who is responsible for the work. Use them to separate teams to control access and operations.

**Territories** define where the work happens. Use them to route work geographically to optimize scheduling and travel.

Combined together, they provide dispatchers with the correct resources.

For example, David is a technician who works for Contoso Services, which is an HVAC and plumbing company in the Seattle area. David's organizational unit is the HVAC division and territory is the Bellevue region.

Avery is the dispatcher for Contoso Services and uses both organizational units and territories to assign work to the appropriate technicians.
