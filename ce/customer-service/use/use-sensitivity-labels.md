# Use sensitivity labels in Dynamics 365 Customer Service emails

Sensitivity labels help you classify and protect business data. When you send or receive emails in Dynamics 365 Customer Service, sensitivity labels make sure your communications follow your organization’s security and compliance policies.

As a customer service representative, you’ll see sensitivity label options directly in the email composer. These labels help protect sensitive information, prevent accidental sharing, and ensure that attachments and replies remain consistent with organizational rules.

## How sensitivity labels work in your emails

When you compose or respond to emails in Dynamics 365 Customer Service, sensitivity labels may be applied automatically, suggested to you, or required by policy. Here’s what you can expect:

- If you apply labels such as **Do Not Forward** or **Do Not Reply** to an email, the corresponding actions are automatically restricted for recipients.
- When you reply to or forward a labeled email, the system automatically applies the same label to your email. You can’t change the current label to a less restrictive label.
- Your organization may have set up a **default sensitivity label**. This label is automatically applied when you start composing a new email in Customer Service. 
- Based on your organization's configurations, you won’t be able to send or even save an email until you’ve applied the required sensitivity label. 
- When you add an attachment to an email, the attachment keeps its existing sensitivity label. If there’s a conflict between the attachment’s label and the email’s label, you’ll see guidance in the email composer about which label should take priority. This ensures the entire message is classified consistently.
- Next to the list of available sensitivity labels, you might see a **Learn more** link. Selecting this opens resources created by your organization to help you understand how to use labels properly and why they matter.

## Working with encrypted emails

Some sensitivity labels apply encryption to emails, which means only authorized recipients can read them.

- If you receive an encrypted email in Customer Service, you’ll be able to view it in the app, or you may be redirected to Outlook on the web (OWA) if additional actions are required.  
- When you send an encrypted email, the label ensures only the intended recipients can open it. Recipients outside your organization might have restricted access depending on policy.  
- Encrypted emails may have special handling rules. For example, even if emails are normally deleted after sync, encrypted messages might stay in place to prevent data loss.


## What this means for you

As a customer service representative, sensitivity labels work in the background to:

- Protect customer data and prevent accidental sharing.
- Ensure attachments and replies carry the right classification.
- Apply the correct default or required label automatically.
- Block unsafe actions like forwarding sensitive information.

You don’t need to configure labels yourself—your organization sets the rules. Your responsibility is simply to choose the right label (if required) when sending emails and to be aware of the restrictions that come with it.
